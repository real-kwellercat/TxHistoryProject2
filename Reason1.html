How was texas Effected by Pearl Harbor???

The American naval base in Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, was the target of a surprise attack by the Empire of Japan on December 7, 1941. Over 2,400 Americans lost their lives as a result of the attack, which also caused significant damage to the U.S. Pacific Fleet and the sinking of several battleships. The attack marked a turning point for the US as it triggered their involvement in World War II.

Prior to the attack on Pearl Harbor, the United States had a policy of neutrality in the conflict. The day after the attack, President Franklin D. Roosevelt spoke to Congress, calling it "a date which will live in infamy" and asking for a war declaration against Japan. After that, Japan's allies Germany and Italy went to war.

Many Texans volunteered to join the military in response to the call for troops. Texas played a significant role in the war effort, with over 750,000 Texans serving in the military over the course of the conflict. This was more than any other state, and a sizable portion of the soldiers serving in the Pacific Theater were from Texas.

Overall, the attack on Pearl Harbor was a turning point in American history and had a significant impact on how World War II played out. The bravery and sacrifice of Texas residents and other Americans who participated in the war effort will always be remembered.
