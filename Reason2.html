How did texas get effected by troops in training?

Texas developed into a center for military training and war-related industries during World War II, drawing farmers and citizens of small towns to create urban centers.

The Great Depression was forgotten as the state saw a 33% increase in population, becoming more urban than rural. More than 750,000 Texans fought in the war, including notable generals like Adm. Chester W. Nimitz and Gen. Dwight D. Eisenhower.

Texas had 142 significant military installations. Five of the thirty-three Texans who received the Medal of Honor were of Latino descent, along with Audie Murphy and Cmdr. Samuel Dealey. Unfortunatelly, more than 22,000 Texans perished in the conflict.
